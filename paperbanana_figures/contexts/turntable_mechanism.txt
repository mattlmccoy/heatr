Discrete turntable model for RFAM anisotropy averaging:

Physical idea:
- Electrodes remain stationary.
- Entire part assembly rotates in discrete angle steps.
- Fields are remapped after each rotation event.

Rotation remap equation:
f_new(x,z) = f_old(R_{-DeltaTheta}(x,z))
for fields f in {temperature T, relative density rho_rel, melt fraction phi_melt}.

Schedule:
t_k = (k/(N_r+1))*t_total, k = 1..N_r
Example used in manuscript:
- N_r = 4
- t_total = 360 s
- Events at t = 72, 144, 216, 288 s
- DeltaTheta = 90 deg each, total 360 deg

Communicative intent:
- Show why frequent early rotation outperforms one late rotation.
- Emphasize finite-step residual anisotropy versus ideal continuous averaging limit.
- Include side-by-side mini timelines:
  A) single 90 deg at 180 s (weak improvement)
  B) four 90 deg steps (stronger uniformity).
