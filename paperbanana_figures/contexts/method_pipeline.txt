RFAM workflow for geometry correction in graphite-doped PA12 powder:

1) Input target polygon T in x-z plane (2D cross-section).
2) Rasterize polygon mask on simulation grid.
3) Solve electro-quasi-static (EQS) problem to obtain electric potential |V| and electric field E.
4) Compute volumetric RF heating rate Q_rf proportional to sigma*|E|^2 and calibrated coupling factor eta.
5) Advance transient thermal state T(x,z,t) with conduction and source Q_rf.
6) Update melt fraction phi_melt and relative density rho_rel with temperature-dependent kinetics.
7) Predict boundary shrinkage from rho_rel sampled near boundary and effective shrinkage length L_eff.
8) Compare predicted sintered boundary B_hat to target T using edge placement error (EPE).
9) Apply boundary correction (prewarp) and optionally turntable schedule, then re-simulate.
10) Stop when RMS(EPE) is below tolerance.
