1. Electro-Quasi-Static (EQS) Problem

HEATR solves an RF electro-quasi-static boundary-value problem in the chamber domain with spatially varying conductivity $\sigma(\mathbf{x})$ and permittivity $\epsilon(\mathbf{x})$.

The complex potential $V(\mathbf{x})$ satisfies:

$$ \nabla \cdot \left[\left(\sigma + j\omega\epsilon\right)\nabla V\right] = 0 $$

with Dirichlet boundary conditions at driven and grounded electrodes. The electric field is $\mathbf{E} = -\nabla V$.

2. RF Power Deposition

Volumetric RF heating is computed from the field magnitude in conductive media:

$$ Q_{\mathrm{rf}}(\mathbf{x}) = \frac{1}{2}\,\sigma(\mathbf{x})\,\left|\mathbf{E}(\mathbf{x})\right|^2 $$

where the factor $\tfrac{1}{2}$ is the time-average for sinusoidal fields. In generator-enforced mode, the computed pattern is scaled to target absorbed power:

$$ \int_{\Omega_{\mathrm{part}}} Q_{\mathrm{rf}}\,dV = \eta\,P_{\mathrm{gen}} $$

with transfer efficiency $\eta$ and source power $P_{\mathrm{gen}}$.

3. Transient Thermal + Phase Model

The temperature field evolves by conduction, RF source, and boundary losses:

$$ \rho c_p\frac{\partial T}{\partial t} = \nabla\cdot(k\nabla T) + Q_{\mathrm{rf}} - Q_{\mathrm{conv}} $$

where convection appears on selected open boundaries as:

$$ -k\nabla T\cdot\hat{n} = h\,(T - T_{\infty}) $$

HEATR exposes two phase-change families in the GUI:

  1. baseline: COMSOL-style smoothed Heaviside apparent heat-capacity transition around a nominal phase window.
  2. experimental_pa12_hybrid: DSC-calibrated apparent heat-capacity melt interval for PA12.

In both cases, melt fraction is represented as:

$$ \phi(T) \in [0,1],\qquad \phi \approx 0 \text{ (solid)},\ \phi \approx 1 \text{ (liquid)} $$

and latent heat is integrated in the effective heat capacity over the transition interval.

Default experimental DSC profile (from configs/experimental_pa12_dsc_profile.yaml):

$$ T_{\mathrm{onset}} = 171.0^\circ\mathrm{C},\quad T_{\mathrm{peak}} = 180.8^\circ\mathrm{C},\quad T_{\mathrm{end}} = 186.0^\circ\mathrm{C},\quad L = 101.7\ \mathrm{kJ/kg} $$

4. Densification Evolution

Relative density $\rho_{rel}$ evolves with temperature and phase-dependent kinetics:

$$ \frac{d\rho_{rel}}{dt} = R_{ss}(T,\phi,\rho_{rel}) + R_{liq}(T,\phi,\rho_{rel}) $$

Model family mapping:

  1. baseline: existing simplified/legacy densification branch for continuity with prior studies.
  2. experimental_pa12_hybrid: viscous-capillary liquid-regime densification with configurable viscosity law (Arrhenius/WLF), plus optional crystallization kinetics branch.

Both paths retain stability controls (thresholding, clipping, and timestep limits).

5. Turntable and Optimizer Logic

Turntable mode rotates field orientation in phases when a trigger metric (e.g., mean melt fraction) is reached. Optimizer mode captures snapshots at requested criteria:

$$ \phi_{mean}(t) \ge \{\phi_1,\phi_2,\ldots\},\qquad T_{max}(t) < T_{ceiling} $$

producing recommended exposure windows and report artifacts.

6. Numerical Pipeline (per run)

  1. Build geometry, masks, materials, and electrodes.
  2. Solve EQS field and compute $Q_{rf}$.
  3. Scale source to generator model (if enabled).
  4. March thermal/phase/density state in time.
  5. Re-solve EQS periodically (or by phase schedule).
  6. Write summaries, plots, and time-series outputs.

7. Notes on Interpretation

The model is a COMSOL-like electrothermal surrogate for rapid iteration. Accuracy depends on geometry fidelity, material calibration, boundary assumptions, and selected numerical settings ($\Delta t$, grid, source constraints).

The browser now defaults to dsc_calibrated_pa12 (internal family key: experimental_pa12_hybrid) but keeps baseline selectable for legacy comparison and reproducibility.

8. Energy Balance Formulation & Validation

HEATR tracks cumulative energy conservation at every outer time step using an incremental energy accumulator that is consistent with the Forward Euler PDE time-integration scheme.

8.1 Conservation identity

At every simulated time $t$, the following identity must hold:

$$ E_{\mathrm{in}}(t) \;=\; E_{\mathrm{stored}}(t) \;+\; E_{\mathrm{out}}(t) \;+\; \varepsilon(t) $$
  • $E_{\mathrm{in}}$ — cumulative RF energy deposited in the doped region $\left[\mathrm{J/m}\right]$
  • $E_{\mathrm{stored}}$ — total internal energy increase in the domain (sensible + latent) $\left[\mathrm{J/m}\right]$
  • $E_{\mathrm{out}}$ — cumulative boundary losses (convection + depth correction) $\left[\mathrm{J/m}\right]$
  • $\varepsilon$ — residual; target $|\varepsilon| / E_{\mathrm{in}} < 1\%$

8.2 Incremental stored-energy accumulator

At each outer time step $n$, material properties are evaluated at the beginning-of-step (BOS) state $(T^n,\, \phi^n,\, \rho_{\mathrm{rel}}^n)$. The energy increment is:

$$ \Delta E^n_{\mathrm{stored}} = \sum_{i,j} \Bigl[ \rho_{\mathrm{eff},ij}^n \, c_{p,\mathrm{eff},ij}^n \,\Delta T_{ij} \;+\; \rho_{\mathrm{eff},ij}^n \, L \,\Delta\phi_{ij} \Bigr]\,\Delta A $$

where $\rho_{\mathrm{eff}} = (1-\phi)\rho_s(\rho_{\mathrm{rel}}) + \phi\rho_{\ell}$ is the blended density at the BOS phase fraction, and $L$ is the latent heat of fusion. Accumulated over all steps:

$$ E_{\mathrm{stored}}(t) = \sum_{n=1}^{N(t)} \Delta E^n_{\mathrm{stored}} $$

This approach is consistent with the Forward Euler time-stepping scheme: both the PDE and the energy tracker evaluate $\rho_{\mathrm{eff}}$ and $c_{p,\mathrm{eff}}$ at the beginning of each time step, ensuring that no artificial energy is introduced or removed by the discretisation.

8.3 Validation report panels

Every run produces a Validation Report (validation_report.png) with six diagnostic panels:

Panel Title What it checks Acceptance criterion
[0,0] Cumulative energy balance [kJ/m] Plots $E_{\mathrm{in}}$, $E_{\mathrm{out}}$, $E_{\mathrm{stored}}$, and the closure sum $E_{\mathrm{stored}} + E_{\mathrm{out}}$ on a single axis. Visual overlap of the closure sum with $E_{\mathrm{in}}$ confirms energy conservation. Closure curve visually indistinguishable from $E_{\mathrm{in}}$ curve.
[0,1] Energy balance residual Left axis: absolute residual $\varepsilon(t)$ [J/m]. Right axis: relative residual $\varepsilon / E_{\mathrm{in}} \times 100$ [%]. Dashed reference lines at $\pm 1\%$ and $\pm 5\%$. Residual growth during rapid phase change reflects Forward Euler outer-step integration error and is expected. $|\varepsilon| / E_{\mathrm{in}} < 1\%$ throughout the run.
[1,0] RF power & generator control Confirms that the RF power deposited in the doped region ($P_{\mathrm{RF}}$) tracks the target power $P_{\mathrm{target}} = \eta \cdot P_{\mathrm{gen}}$ at every step. The QRF scale factor quantifies the multiplicative correction applied to the raw EQS field to enforce the generator power constraint. $P_{\mathrm{RF}} \approx P_{\mathrm{target}}$ at all times; scale factor constant (field geometry unchanged between EQS updates).
[1,1] Numerical stability (ΔT per outer step) Maximum temperature change across all cells per outer time step, before and after CFL sub-step clipping. A large gap between raw and used ΔT indicates that the CFL limiter is frequently active, which may introduce additional numerical diffusion. The clipped-cell fraction quantifies how many cells are affected. $\Delta T_{\mathrm{used}} \ll T_{\mathrm{pc}}$; clipped fraction near zero after the initial transient.
[2,0] Temperature statistics & uniformity Mean and maximum part temperature over time (left axis), and the uniformity indices UIabs and UIrms (right axis). UI is defined as the normalised deviation from mean temperature: $\mathrm{UI_{abs}} = \overline{|T - \bar{T}|} / (\bar{T} - T_\infty)$. Low UI at high $\bar{T}$ confirms spatially uniform heating. UI < 0.05 by end of exposure; $T_{\max} - \bar{T}$ limited.
[2,1] Densification kinetics Mean melt fraction $\bar{\phi}$ and mean relative density $\bar{\rho}_{\mathrm{rel}}$ (left axis). Densification rate $d\rho_{\mathrm{rel}}/dt$ and liquid-regime kinetic coefficient $k_{\mathrm{dens,liq}}$ (right axis, [s$^{-1}$]). Together these confirm that the sintering kinetics are physically plausible and that densification accelerates only after significant melting. Monotonic $\bar{\rho}_{\mathrm{rel}}$ increase; $k_{\mathrm{dens,liq}}$ non-zero only when $\bar{\phi} \gtrsim 0.01$.
